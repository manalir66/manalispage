Fusing Signals, Informing Sight:
Research in Image Processing and Medical Vision

I am a Postdoctoral Research Scientist at the School of Medical Science and Technology, IIT Kharagpur, currently leading the ICMR-funded First-in-World Grand Challenge project titled "Multimodal Fusion of Tear Fern Patterns and Fundus Imaging for Glaucoma Detection in Low-resource Settings." My doctoral work at IIT(ISM) Dhanbad focused on developing classical image fusion algorithms in spatial and transform domains — covering multi-focus, infrared-visible, and low-light imaging. I work at the intersection of morphological image and signal processing, sparse representation, and applied machine learning, with a focus on interpretable and computationally efficient methods.

Research Focus
Multimodal Glaucoma Detection in Low-resource Settings
Leading an ICMR-funded Grand Challenge integrating Tear Fern crystallography patterns with Fundus imaging. Involves field data collection from rural communities, multimodal dataset curation, and device prototyping.
Medical Imaging · Multimodal Fusion
Image Fusion in Spatial & Transform Domains
Developed DCT-based multiscale frameworks and morphological differential features for greyscale and colour image fusion. Covers wavelet morphology, toggle-contrast edge features, and vision-inspired IR-visible fusion.
DCT · Morphology · Wavelet
Multi-focus & Colour Image Fusion
Designed edge-based fusion schemes using morphological toggle-gradient and guided filters. Extended to DTT-moment reorganisation and sparse representation for challenging multi-focus scenarios.
Sparse Representation · Guided Filter
Low-light Image Enhancement
PSO-based gamma correction with image classification feedback and multi-scale pyramidal fusion for correcting low-illumination scenes — enabling downstream vision tasks in degraded conditions.
PSO · Gamma Correction · Multi-scale
